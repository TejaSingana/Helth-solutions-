Diabetes Information


Diabetes is a condition characterized by increased levels of sugar in the blood. It can lead to various symptoms and complications if not managed properly. Common symptoms include increased thirst, unexplained weight loss, blurred vision, and slow healing of wounds. Managing diabetes involves maintaining a healthy diet, monitoring blood sugar levels, and regular exercise.

Symptoms

  • Increased thirst
  • Excessive hunger
  • Frequent urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow healing of wounds
  • Fatigue
  • Numbness or tingling in hands or feet
  • Recurrent infections
  • Irritability

Precautions

  • Maintain a healthy diet
  • Regularly monitor blood sugar levels
  • Engage in regular physical activity
  • Avoid excessive consumption of sugary foods
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol intake
  • Take medications as prescribed

Medications

  • Metformin
  • Insulin (Various types)
  • Sulfonylureas (e.g., Glipizide, Glyburide)
  • DPP-4 inhibitors (e.g., Sitagliptin)
  • GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., Liraglutide)
  • SGLT2 inhibitors (e.g., Empagliflozin)
  • Thiazolidinediones (e.g., Pioglitazone)
